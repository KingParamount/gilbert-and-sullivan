# The Learn-O-Matic — a gift for the Society

## What is it?

A free, friendly website that helps singers **learn their part** for any Gilbert
& Sullivan opera. A member chooses their part (say, *Alto*, or *Ko-Ko*), then a
song, and the tool plays the **piano accompaniment with their own line on top**,
with the words highlighting karaoke-style as the music goes. They can slow it
down to learn tricky passages, loop around, or bring in all the other parts to
hear how it fits together.

It covers **all thirteen Savoy operas** — from *Trial by Jury* to *The Grand
Duke* — nearly 300 musical numbers in total.

## Who is it for?

Everyone in the company, especially:
- **Chorus members** drilling their SATB line.
- **Principals** learning a song before music calls.
- Anyone who can't read music fluently and learns best by **listening and
  following the words**.

It works on a computer, phone or tablet — so people can practise on the bus, at
the kitchen table, or backstage.

## How do people use it?

1. Open the website.
2. Pick the opera from the drop-down at the top.
3. Tap your **part**, then the **song** — only the numbers you actually sing in
   appear.
4. Press **Play**. Slow it down, mute the piano, or "Play All Parts" as you wish.

No login, no app to install, nothing to pay.

## What does the Society need to do to host it?

Very little. It's an ordinary website (a folder of files), so your webmaster can
put it online in a few minutes — see **`INSTALL.md`** for step-by-step options
(upload to your existing site, add it to a WordPress menu, or use free hosting).
There's no software to install on a server and nothing to maintain.

There's also a **ready-to-print rehearsal poster** with a QR code in the
`poster/` folder — pin it up and members can scan straight to the tool.
